# Vorsignale (Vr) — Distant signals

Distant signals (*Vorsignale*) stand ahead of a main signal, normally at braking distance, and announce the aspect the driver is to expect there. They exist as fixed *Formvorsignale* (a hinged yellow disc, optionally with a movable arm, plus two lights rising to the right at night), as *Lichtvorsignale* (two lights rising to the right), and as manually-set *Wärtervorsignale*. Reading the distant signal never relieves the driver of the duty to observe the main signal itself.

**Intro — ESO (14) / § 4 (1):** *Vorsignale zeigen an, welches Signalbild am zugehörigen Hauptsignal zu erwarten ist.* — "Distant signals indicate which aspect is to be expected at the associated main signal." Signal **Vr 0** may also announce a protection signal (**Sh 0** and **Sh 2**), and per AB 16 / § 4 (1a) it may announce **Hp 0** at a blocking signal (colour-light).

Per ESO 16 / § 4 (1d), distant signals normally stand at the line's braking distance ahead of the associated signal; where they stand closer, this is specially indicated.

**Vorsignalwiederholer (distant-signal repeater) — AB 25 / § 4 (3a):** where the view of the main signal is obstructed, the distant signal may be repeated as a colour-light signal (a *Vorsignalwiederholer*). Light distant signals standing more than 5% closer than the line braking distance carry a white supplementary light above the left signal light, roughly level with the right one. The repeater shows the same picture but is not fitted with a distant-signal board (*Vorsignaltafel*) and is not announced by distant-signal markers (*Vorsignalbaken*); repeaters not identified by the supplementary light carry a rectangular white board with a black border and black ring on the mast. Light distant signals at the site of a light main signal are dark when they do not apply to the set route or when the main signal shows **Hp 0** (AB 28 / § 4 (3b)).

### `Vr 0` — Halt erwarten · *Expect stop*

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Signalbegriff** | Vr 0 |
| **German title** | Halt erwarten |
| **English** | Expect stop |
| **Form** | Formsignal + Lichtsignal |
| **DS 301 ref** | ESO (17) |
| **DV 301 ref** | § 4 (5), (5a) |
| **Source** | SB-DBAG 2006 p.10 |

**Signalbild (DE):** *Formsignal:* Eine runde, gelbe Scheibe mit schwarzem Ring und weißem Rand steht senkrecht. Wo ein Flügel vorhanden ist, zeigt er mit der Spitze senkrecht nach unten. *Bei Dunkelheit und Lichtsignal:* Zwei gelbe Lichter nach rechts steigend.

**Signal picture (EN):** *Semaphore:* a round yellow disc with a black ring and white border stands vertical (upright). Where an arm is fitted, its tip points vertically downward. *In darkness and as a colour-light signal:* two yellow lights rising to the right.

**Bedeutung (DE):** Halt erwarten — am zugehörigen Hauptsignal ist »Hp 0« zu erwarten. An Vorsignalen, die nicht an Hauptsignalen stehen, kann bis auf weiteres nur ein gelbes Licht gezeigt werden.

**Meaning (EN):** Expect stop — expect **Hp 0** at the associated main signal (Vr 0 may also announce Sh 0 / Sh 2, or Hp 0 at a blocking signal). At distant signals not standing at main signals, only a single yellow light may be shown until further notice.

**Maps to →** family `vorsignale` · variant `formvorsignal` + `lichtvorsignal` · aspect concept `caution`. Panels: `arm` (kind `distant`; upright yellow disc, arm tip down) and/or `lamps` (two yellow lamps rising to the right). Lamp colours: `yellow`.

### `Vr 2` — Langsamfahrt erwarten · *Expect slow speed*

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Signalbegriff** | Vr 2 |
| **German title** | Langsamfahrt erwarten |
| **English** | Expect slow speed |
| **Form** | Formsignal + Lichtsignal |
| **DS 301 ref** | ESO (17)–(18) |
| **DV 301 ref** | § 4 (10), (10a) |
| **Source** | SB-DBAG 2006 p.11 |

**Signalbild (DE):** *Formsignal:* Eine runde, gelbe Scheibe mit schwarzem Ring und weißem Rand steht senkrecht. Der Flügel zeigt schräg nach rechts abwärts. *Bei Dunkelheit und Lichtsignal:* Ein gelbes Licht und nach rechts steigend ein grünes Licht.

**Signal picture (EN):** *Semaphore:* a round yellow disc with a black ring and white border stands upright. The arm points diagonally downward to the right. *In darkness and as a colour-light signal:* one yellow light and, rising to the right, one green light.

**Bedeutung (DE):** Langsamfahrt erwarten — am zugehörigen Hauptsignal ist »Hp 2« zu erwarten. Das Signal kann auch ein grünes Licht und nach rechts steigend ein gelbes Licht zeigen.

**Meaning (EN):** Expect slow speed — expect **Hp 2** at the associated main signal. The signal may also show a green light with a yellow light rising to the right (the reverse order).

**Maps to →** family `vorsignale` · variant `formvorsignal` + `lichtvorsignal` · aspect concept `expect-slow`. Panels: `arm` (kind `distant`; upright disc, arm inclined down-right) and/or `lamps` (yellow + green rising to the right; may be reversed to green + yellow). Lamp colours: `yellow`, `green`.
