# German Railway Signal Book (DS/DV 301)

## Introduction

> **Translator's note**
>
> This document is an English translation of the 2006 Deutsche Bahn Signal Book. Signal names (for example, `Hp 0`, `Vr 0`, `Zs 1`) are retained in German as they are official designations. The translation has been written to read naturally in English while preserving the intent of the original regulations. References to BahnStatistik.de have been removed.

## Overview

The Joint Signal Book (SB) DS/DV 301 combines the signalling regulations of the former Deutsche Bundesbahn (DB) and the former Deutsche Reichsbahn (DR). It is primarily intended for railway staff whose duties require knowledge of both rulebooks.

The book contains:

* the principal provisions governing railway signals used by Deutsche Bahn AG;
* the implementation provisions relating to the Railway Signalling Regulations (ESO);
* temporary signalling arrangements that differ from the ESO; and
* additional DB AG instructions, identified as **Supplement** where applicable.

The illustrations are approximately to scale unless clarity required otherwise.

## Scope

The Railway Signalling Regulations apply to both standard and narrow gauge railways, except where specifically excluded. Permanent and temporary local variations are published separately in operating notices, timetables, local instructions and engineering notices.

## Definitions

### Signal

A visible or audible indication conveying defined information to ensure the safe movement of railway vehicles.

### Signal designation

The abbreviated identifier of a signal (for example `Hp 0`).

### Signal meaning

The operational instruction conveyed by the signal.

### Signal aspect

The visual appearance or audible indication that represents the signal.
