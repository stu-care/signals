# Ks repeaters and shortened braking distance

## Why the white light position matters

A Ks signal may use a small white supplementary light for two different player-relevant purposes. The main Ks colour still carries the actual proceed or warning information. The white light explains the context.

| White light position | Meaning | What the player should remember |
|---|---|---|
| Above the main lamps | The announced stopping point or speed change is at a shortened braking distance | Act sooner than you would for a normal full-distance warning |
| Below the main lamps | The signal repeats distant information already shown earlier | Do not treat the repeater as the start of a fresh full braking distance |

## Distant-signal repeater

A repeater is used when visibility of the next main signal needs improving. It repeats information already given by an earlier signal.

For Ks, the lower white supplementary light is shown with a restrictive repeated indication. It is not normally illuminated with a plain steady Ks 1 indication.

The site should therefore describe the full display as, for example:

- `Ks 2 + repeater marker`
- `flashing Ks 1 + Zs 3v + repeater marker`

It should not create a fictitious independent aspect called “white repeater light”.

## Shortened braking distance

The upper white supplementary light warns that the distance to the announced stopping point is more than five per cent shorter than the route's normal braking distance.

This can accompany Ks 2 or a speed announcement. The practical message for a player is simple: there is less room than usual to complete the required braking.

## Data modelling rule

Both uses share one white-light SVG component. Their records differ by:

- placement;
- meaning;
- compatible aspects;
- player action;
- route evidence.
